## Investment Impact

● **Services crossed the $100B milestone — structural re-rating catalyst**

Q4 FY2025 Services revenue of $28.748B marked an all-time quarterly record, +15.1% YoY, and pushed full-year FY2025 Services revenue above $100B for the first time in Apple's history. Growth was broad-based across the App Store, Apple Music, iCloud+, Apple TV+, and Apple Pay, reflecting organic expansion across the installed base of over 2.35 billion active devices rather than any single product event. Critically, Services surpassed iPhone as Apple's largest gross profit contributor in a single quarter for the first time in company history — a structural inflection that validates the long-held thesis that Apple is transitioning from a hardware-cycle-dependent company to a recurring-revenue platform. At a 75.3% segment gross margin versus Products at 36.2%, every incremental dollar of Services mix shift adds approximately 39 cents of gross profit per dollar of revenue that a product dollar cannot. With the Services segment now above $100B in annualized revenue and still growing at a mid-teens rate, the investment case for a blended margin re-rating is no longer speculative — it is materializing in reported numbers quarter by quarter.

● **Gross margin expansion — 47.2% beats guidance ceiling by 20 bps**

Apple reported Q4 FY2025 gross margin of 47.2%, beating the analyst consensus of 46.5% by 70 basis points and clearing the high end of the company's own guided 46–47% range by 20 basis points. On a YoY basis, gross margin expanded 100 basis points from 46.2% in Q4 FY2024, with Services mix shift contributing approximately 95 basis points of the improvement. Notably, this margin achievement was realized despite absorbing a $1.1B tariff-related cost headwind in the quarter — an indication of the structural, not cyclical, nature of the underlying margin expansion. The divergence between Products gross margin (36.2%) and Services gross margin (75.3%) reinforces that incremental mix toward software and services is the primary earnings-per-share lever for the next several fiscal years. For Q1 FY2026, management guided gross margin of 47–48%, sustaining and potentially extending current levels; at the midpoint of 47.5%, that would represent an additional 30 basis points of sequential improvement even with a $1.4B estimated tariff headwind. The forward margin guide is a direct input to our revised FY2026E EPS of $7.90.

● **Q1 FY2026 guidance of +10–12% YoY — materially above Street expectations**

Management guided Q1 FY2026 revenue growth of +10–12% YoY, implying a revenue range of $136.7–139.2B. Against the Street consensus of approximately $130B at the time of the print, the midpoint of guidance exceeds buy-side expectations by roughly $7.4B, or approximately +6%. The step-up from Q4's +8.0% YoY growth rate reflects several converging tailwinds: the iPhone 17 lineup, which Cook described as the strongest in company history, with supply constraints on multiple models indicating demand-constrained rather than supply-push dynamics; Apple Intelligence features expanding to additional languages and geographies, with management noting measurable lift in purchase consideration in surveyed markets; and a return to growth expected in Greater China, where Q4's -3.6% decline was attributable to iPhone 17 production timing rather than competitive erosion. Apple has established a pattern of conservative initial quarterly guidance — the company has beaten its own revenue midpoint in 18 of the last 20 quarters — suggesting the $136.7–139.2B range represents a floor more than a ceiling.

● **Maintaining BUY; raising price target to $256 from $240**

Our revised price target of $256 is derived from a blended methodology: 33x our FY2026E EPS of $7.90 yields $261; 22x forward EV/EBITDA yields $236; and a discounted cash flow model using a 9.5% WACC and 3.0% terminal growth rate yields $272. The blended average of $256 implies 12.3% upside from the $228 report-date closing price. The revision from $240 reflects an upgrade to our FY2026E EPS from $7.55 to $7.90, driven by gross margin expansion and Services revenue outperformance. The investment thesis is strengthened by this quarter's results: Services scale, margin durability, and above-consensus guidance collectively reduce the bear case that Apple is a maturing hardware company. Key risks that could impair this thesis include: escalation of tariffs beyond the currently modeled $1.4B Q1 headwind, competitive pressure in Greater China from Huawei and domestic AI-enabled handsets, and a potential slowdown in App Store revenue growth subject to ongoing regulatory scrutiny in the EU and the United States.

## Section 2: Segment Analysis — iPhone Miss Masked by Services Milestone

### iPhone: Transitional Miss on Supply, Not Demand

iPhone revenue of $49.0B (+6.1% YoY) missed consensus by $1.16B (-2.3%), yet the shortfall is transitional rather than structural. Cook was explicit on the earnings call: "We're constrained today on several models of the iPhone 17. There's not a ramp issue. It's just we have very strong demand." The iPhone 17 Pro launched as the most capable device in the lineup's history, and supply constraints across multiple SKUs — not weakening consumer appetite — account for the delta versus the $50.19B Street estimate. Cook characterized the lineup as "our strongest ever," and forward channel checks support a Q1 FY2026 catch-up as production scales.

### Mac: Beat on M-Series Refresh Cycle

Mac delivered $8.7B (+13.0% YoY), beating consensus by $140M (+1.6%). The outperformance reflects the M-series chip refresh cycle driving both consumer and enterprise upgrades. At a 13% YoY growth rate against a comparable quarter that itself benefited from prior-generation launches, Mac's trajectory confirms sustained pricing power in the premium PC segment.

### iPad: In Line; Steady Contribution

iPad revenue of $7.0B (+4.9% YoY) came in essentially at consensus ($6.98B, +0.3% delta). The result reflects stable demand without a major new product catalyst in the quarter. iPad remains a reliable mid-tier contributor with no thesis-moving variance.

### Wearables, Home & Accessories: Beat Against a Bearish Bar

Wearables revenue of $9.0B was roughly flat YoY (-0.2%) but beat a notably low consensus of $8.49B by $520M (+6.1%). Apple Watch Series 10 and AirPods 4 launches drove the upside. The segment's near-zero YoY growth rate reflects the broader wearables maturation curve, but the consensus beat signals that the Street had over-discounted competition risk.

### Services: Record Revenue; Historic Gross Profit Inflection

Services delivered $28.75B (+15.1% YoY), beating consensus by $578M (+2.0%) and setting an all-time quarterly record. CFO Kevan Parekh described it as "really organically driven broad-based double-digit growth across most markets and categories." At a 75.3% gross margin, Services generated approximately $21.6B in gross profit this quarter. This compares to approximately $26.7B of gross profit from the entire Products segment ($73.8B × 36.2%) — meaning Services, at 28% of revenue, is rapidly converging on Products as the dominant gross profit engine. FY2025 marks the first full fiscal year in which Apple's Services segment crossed $100B in annual revenue, a structural milestone that reshapes the company's earnings quality profile permanently.

## Section 3: Geographic Performance — Europe and India Power Diversification

Q4 FY2025 confirmed that Apple's geographic diversification thesis is actively reducing concentration risk. Four of five regions grew, with total revenue reaching $102.5B (+8.0% YoY). Europe was the standout at +13.4% YoY ($28.7B), validating premium-tier price elasticity in developed markets. The Rest of Asia Pacific region — led by India — surged +25.7% to $8.4B, the fastest growth of any geography and a structural signal of an emerging demand engine. Americas, the largest region at $44.2B, grew a steady +6.0%. Greater China was the sole decliner at -3.6% ($14.5B vs. $15.0B), but Cook attributed the shortfall explicitly to iPhone 16 demand forecast mismatches and supply timing rather than deteriorating brand equity or demand destruction.

*Source: [Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Release](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2025/10/apple-reports-fourth-quarter-results/); Q4 FY2024 from [Apple Q4 FY2024 Earnings Release](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2024/10/apple-reports-fourth-quarter-results/)*

**Greater China (-3.6%):** The $536M YoY revenue decline in Greater China is the quarter's most scrutinized datapoint, but the operative context is supply timing, not demand erosion. Cook noted that iPhone 17 demand forecasts for China were initially set conservatively in the wake of the iPhone 16 cycle; inventory allocation was subsequently insufficient to meet in-store traffic that was up significantly YoY. Cook conducted an in-person visit to China and guided explicitly: "The iPhone 17 has been very well received there. We do believe that we'll return to growth in Q1." Competition from Huawei in the premium domestic segment remains a structural watch item, but Q4's miss does not alter the medium-term China thesis.

**Rest of Asia Pacific / India (+25.7%):** The region's $1.7B YoY revenue gain to $8.4B represents the most dynamic geographic growth vector in Apple's portfolio. India is the primary driver, with Apple executing across three simultaneous expansion vectors: domestic manufacturing (reducing geopolitical supply concentration), new retail stores in tier-1 cities, and a developer ecosystem buildout. At +25.7%, the region is growing at more than three times the company average, and from an increasingly meaningful base.

**Europe (+13.4%):** Europe's $3.4B YoY revenue gain to $28.7B reflects sustained consumer willingness to absorb premium pricing in a macro environment that has pressured discretionary spending broadly. Digital Markets Act (DMA) compliance requirements continue to introduce operational complexity — alternative app marketplace obligations in particular — but there is no visible revenue impairment from regulatory friction in the quarterly result.

**Thesis Implications:** Services now generates gross profit at a 75.3% margin and is the fastest-growing segment by revenue — the mix shift toward high-margin recurring revenue is the dominant driver of Apple's expanding earnings quality. Geographically, the +25.7% growth in Rest of Asia Pacific and the return-to-growth signal from China in Q1 FY2026 indicate that the multi-regional diversification strategy is effectively reducing single-region concentration risk. Premium pricing integrity across Americas, Europe, and Japan confirms that Apple's brand moat remains structurally intact.

## Section 4: Margin Analysis — 47.2% Gross Margin Beats Guidance Ceiling; Services Mix Now Dominant Profit Driver

Apple's Q4 FY2025 consolidated gross margin of 47.2% exceeded the high end of the 46–47% guidance range by 20 basis points, expanded 100 bps year-over-year from 46.2%, and came in 70 bps above the Street consensus. In a landmark structural shift, Services gross profit surpassed iPhone gross profit for the first time in company history, marking a pivotal inflection in Apple's profit architecture.

| Products Gross Margin | 36.2% | ~35.0%E | ~+120 bps E | — |
| Services Gross Margin | 75.3% | ~74.5%E | ~+80 bps E | — |
| Operating Margin (Est.) | 31.7% | ~31.1%E | +60 bps | — |
| Net Margin | 26.8% | 15.5%* | nm* | — |

*Q4 FY2024 net margin of 15.5% depressed by $10.2B one-time EU State Aid tax charge; underlying net margin expansion ~+60–80 bps YoY*
*Source: [Apple 10-K FY2025](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/320193/000032019325000079/aapl-20250927.htm); operating and net margins estimated from reported financials*

**Margin Expansion Drivers.** The 100 bps YoY consolidated GM improvement is attributable primarily to Services mix shift, which contributed an estimated ~95 bps of the total expansion. The mathematical basis is straightforward: Services represented 28.0% of Q4 FY2025 revenue versus approximately 25.5% a year prior — a 2.5 percentage-point shift. Given the 3,910 bps spread between Services GM (75.3%) and Products GM (36.2%), each 1 pp mix shift from Products to Services mechanically adds ~39 bps to the consolidated margin. The 2.5 pp shift therefore contributed ~98 bps, consistent with the reported outcome. Additional tailwinds included operating leverage on a record revenue base and favorable iPhone Pro Max mix within the product segment.

Partially offsetting these tailwinds: approximately $1.1B in tariff-related costs absorbed in the quarter, equivalent to a ~107 bps headwind (without which consolidated GM would have approached ~48.3%); new iPhone 17 production launch costs; elevated full-year R&D spending of $34.5B; and Greater China revenue softness reducing the contribution of high-ASP iPhone Pro variants.

**Products vs. Services Gross Profit Split.** Services generated approximately $21.6B of gross profit at 75.3% GM, while Products contributed approximately $26.7B at 36.2% GM — but the gap is compressing rapidly. Services now constitutes ~44.7% of total gross profit, up from historically below 30%. At the current mix-shift trajectory, Services is on pace to become the majority gross profit contributor within 2–3 quarters, a structural re-rating event that the consensus has not yet fully priced.

This is approximately $8B, or ~6.2%, above the Street's pre-earnings consensus of ~$130B — a magnitude of guidance beat that is unusual for a company of Apple's scale and conservatism.

**Guidance Assessment.** Apple's track record of conservative guidance materially elevates credibility here. In Q4 FY2025, management guided "mid-to-high single digits" revenue growth — the company delivered +8%, at the high end of the stated range; GM guidance of up to 47% was exceeded by 20 bps. Against this backdrop, the Q1 FY2026 guide embedding 47–48% GM and $136.7B–$139.2B revenue should be interpreted as a floor, not a ceiling. Key assumptions embedded in the guide include: fulfillment of iPhone 17 back-order demand across supply-constrained models (Air, Pro Max); a return to growth in Greater China, supported by CEO Tim Cook's personal market visit and materially higher store traffic YoY; and continued double-digit Services growth at approximately 14–15% YoY. ## Section 6: Updated Investment Thesis

### What Has Changed

**1. Services as Primary Profit Engine: STRUCTURALLY STRENGTHENED**

Services crossed the $100B full-year revenue milestone and — most significantly — surpassed iPhone as the largest gross profit contributor for the first time in company history. At 75.3% GM, each incremental dollar of Services revenue generates approximately 39 cents more gross profit than a dollar of Products revenue. This is not a temporary mix phenomenon: Services constituted 28.0% of Q4 FY2025 revenue versus approximately 25.5% a year ago, and management confirmed continued organic double-digit growth extending into Q1 FY2026. The blended margin mathematics are compounding in favor of a structural re-rating of Apple's earnings quality — a Services-majority gross profit profile commands a higher terminal multiple than a hardware-dominated one.

**2. iPhone 17 Supercycle Setup: STRENGTHENED**

Q4 FY2025's -$1.16B iPhone miss versus consensus was supply-timing driven, not demand-driven — CEO Tim Cook explicitly confirmed demand strength across multiple supply-constrained iPhone 17 models on the earnings call. The iPhone 17 lineup (Air, standard 17, Pro, Pro Max) is characterized as the "strongest ever," with the Air representing the first meaningful industrial design evolution in years. Apple Intelligence features are cited as a "growing factor" in upgrade decisions among the installed base. With approximately 900 million iPhone users still on devices four or more years old, the addressable upgrade opportunity entering FY2026 is the largest in the company's history.

**3. Greater China Bear Case: MEANINGFULLY WEAKENED**

Greater China's -3.6% YoY revenue decline in Q4 was attributable to iPhone 16 supply shortfalls — a demand forecast miss — rather than structural demand deterioration or Huawei share recapture. Cook's personal visit to China, combined with verified reports of materially higher Apple Store traffic YoY and strong initial iPhone 17 demand data, provides incremental conviction that Q1 FY2026 will see a return to growth as implied in guidance. Additionally, Greater China's reduced share of total revenue (14.1% in Q4 FY2025 versus 15.8% in Q4 FY2024) partially reduces single-market concentration risk even as the recovery narrative gains momentum.

### What Has NOT Changed

The core thesis pillars remain fully intact. Apple's 2.2B+ active device installed base continues to expand, creating a continuously growing addressable market for Services monetization at structurally high margins. The buyback program — $89.3B returned in FY2025 — mechanically reduces share count and supports per-share earnings growth independent of revenue momentum. Apple's premium pricing power has not been challenged across any product segment. The balance sheet, carrying $132.4B in total cash and investments, remains fortress-grade, providing optionality for accelerated capital return, strategic M&A, or AI infrastructure investment.

### Investment Recommendation

We maintain our **BUY** rating and raise our 12-month price target to **$256** from $240, representing 12.3% implied upside from the October 30, 2025 report-date price of $228. Our blended valuation methodology — 33x FY2026E P/E applied to $7.90 EPS, 22x EV/EBITDA, and a DCF at 9.5% WACC — converges at $256. We note the Street has moved materially more constructive post-earnings, with major bank targets clustering in the $290–$330 range; our estimate may prove conservative if iPhone 17 cycle velocity and Services margin expansion sustain through FY2026 at the pace management's tone implies. We view any near-term tariff-driven pullback as an accumulation opportunity.

We raise our price target to **$256** (from $240), representing **12.3% implied upside** from the October 30, 2025 closing price of **$228.00**, and maintain our **BUY** rating. Our methodology blends three independent valuation approaches — NTM P/E, EV/EBITDA, and DCF — each of which corroborates a materially higher intrinsic value than current trading levels.

**P/E:** At 33x FY2026E EPS of $7.90, Apple trades at a premium to the S&P 500 (~21x) but at a meaningful discount to pure-software peers. The multiple is justified on three grounds. First, Services has crossed $100B in annual revenue at 75%+ gross margin, now representing ~44.7% of total gross profit — a structurally durable, high-multiple earnings stream that continues to re-rate the blended P/E higher. Second, the world's largest share buyback program ($89.3B in FY2025) mechanically supports EPS growth independent of revenue trajectory, providing a floor under per-share earnings. Third, Apple Intelligence represents an unmonetized call option embedded across 2.2B+ active devices — optionality that carries no current revenue credit in our model. Apple's historical P/E range of 25–35x supports the current 33x application.

**EV/EBITDA:** At 22x FY2026E EBITDA of approximately $155B, enterprise value implies approximately $3.41 trillion. Adding $54.1B net cash (total investments of $132.4B minus long-term debt of $78.3B) yields an equity value of approximately $3.46 trillion, and an implied price of approximately $236 on 14.7B diluted shares. The 22x multiple reflects a modest premium to hardware/software hybrid peers and a deliberate discount to pure-software comparables (Microsoft ~24x, Alphabet ~18x), balanced by Apple's growing recurring revenue base and an EBITDA margin trajectory extending toward 35%+.

**DCF:** Using base FCF of $115B (FY2026E; ~26% FCF margin on $438B revenue), growing at a 10% CAGR over five years, with WACC of 9.5% (reflecting Apple's investment-grade credit profile, large-cap status, and low beta) and terminal growth of 3.0% (consistent with nominal GDP plus modest share gains), our intrinsic value range is **$265–$280 per share**. WACC is unchanged from prior estimates. The FCF margin assumption is intentionally conservative relative to FY2025 actuals ($98.8B FCF on $416B revenue = 23.7% margin), with expansion driven by continued Services mix shift.
